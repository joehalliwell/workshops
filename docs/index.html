Welcome! Follow these four(ish) simple(ish) steps to get going!

0. Install Anaconda

With any luck you've installed Anaconda already, but if not or if you had problems with it, one of our volunteers can help!

1. Download the workshop "notebook"

Right-click the relevant link below and "Save link as..." to your desktop, or documents folder. (The weird ".ipynb" extension is correct!)

  • Workshop 1: Introduction to data science and machine learning with Pandas wherein we use pandas, numpy, matplotlib, seaborn and scikit learn to model what it took to survive the wreck of the Titanic
  • Workshop 2: Introduction to Apache Spark's MLLib wherein we use Apache Spark and MLLib to gain insights into the madness of California real estate prices
  • Workshop 3: Topic extraction wherein we use SpaCy and Scikit Learn to cluster a set of documents, learning about document vectors, analogies and 300D glasses along the way

2. Start Jupyter

Anaconda creates entries under the "Start" menu in Windows or the "Applications" folder in OSX. One of them should be for Jupyter notebook.

If you're in Linux, I reckon you've got this, but it'll be something like:

root@elite:~# jupyter notebook

3. Open the notebook in Jupyter

Navigate to the folder where you put the "notebook" you downloaded in step 2 and open it in Jupyter.

4. Have fun!

The notebook will guide you through any additional steps.

What to do if you get stuck

The goal here is to go at your own pace and follow your nose. If you get distracted and end up ignoring the notebook altogether, that is excellent. If you end up reading through loads of background material to really understand something, that is excellent.

But if you're getting stuck in a frustrating way, please holler. Our volunteers (and your neighbours...) are here to help.
